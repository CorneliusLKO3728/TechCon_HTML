Our History

The Beginning (2010)

TechCon was founded in 2010 by a group of passionate technologists who believed in the power of community and knowledge sharing. What started as a small gathering of 200 enthusiasts in a local community center has grown into one of the world's premier technology conferences, attracting thousands of attendees from over 50 countries.

Growth and Evolution (2010-2020)

Over the years, TechCon has evolved from a single-track event to a multi-day conference featuring dozens of speakers, workshops, and networking opportunities. In 2015, we introduced our innovation showcase, allowing startups to present their groundbreaking ideas. By 2018, TechCon had become a must-attend event for anyone in the technology industry, with attendance reaching over 5,000 participants.

Modern Era (2020-Present)

The global pandemic in 2020 challenged us to innovate. We successfully transitioned to a hybrid format, combining in-person experiences with virtual participation, making TechCon accessible to a global audience. This transformation has allowed us to reach over 50,000 virtual attendees while maintaining the intimate, engaging atmosphere that defines our in-person events. Today, TechCon stands at the forefront of technology conferences, continuously adapting to serve our community better.

Our Mission

At TechCon, our mission is to inspire, educate, and connect the global technology community. We believe that innovation thrives when diverse minds come together to share ideas, challenge assumptions, and collaborate on solutions to the world's most pressing technological challenges.

We are committed to creating an inclusive environment where everyone—from students and hobbyists to industry veterans and entrepreneurs—can learn, grow, and contribute to shaping the future of technology. Through our carefully curated content, hands-on workshops, and networking opportunities, we aim to empower attendees with the knowledge and connections they need to drive meaningful change in their organizations and communities.
